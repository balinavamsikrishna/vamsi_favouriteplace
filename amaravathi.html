AMARAVATHI

**IT IS A CAPITAL CITY OF ANDHRAPRADESH**

Capital city of Andhra Pradesh

Amaravati is the capital city of Andhra Pradesh, India. It was founded by N. Chandrababu Naidu and the foundation stone was laid by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2015. The city is spread across 217.23 square kilometers and includes villages from three mandals. The Amaravati Development Corporation Limited and Andhra Pradesh Capital Region Development Authority (APCRDA) are responsible for the city's development.

Mythological city

In Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, Amaravati is the capital city of Svarga, the realm of Indra, the king of the devas. It is also known as Devapura, which means "city of the devas", and Pūṣābhāsā, which means "sun-splendour".

Ancient Stupa

The Amaravati Stupa was built in the second century BCE and third century CE. It is a historic Buddhist site that was unearthed during the construction of the town of Amaravathi.

Amaravathi River

The Amaravathi River flows through the Anjanad valley in Kerala, India and joins the Cauvery River near Karur, Tamil Nadu. The river's name evokes the greenery and Kalpavriksha tree of Lord Indra's heaven.
